    ⚡ TL;DR

    • Technical SEO makes a site crawlable, fast, and understandable to machines.
    • Start with indexing, speed, and structured data — in that order.
    • Audit quarterly; fix what measurably blocks crawling or rendering.

    What does technical SEO cover?

    Technical SEO is the work that makes a website easy for search engines and AI systems to crawl, render, and understand. It covers indexing control, site speed, structured data, and architecture — the foundation content quality stands on. A self-contained answer like this one, placed directly under the question heading, is what AI engines extract.

    Crawling and indexing

    Robots directives, sitemaps, and canonical URLs decide what gets into the index at all.

    Rendering and speed

    Fast first paint and non-blocking scripts measurably increase both rankings and AI citations.

    Technical vs. on-page vs. off-page

    Comparison — the three SEO pillars

    Where should you start?

    Begin with an index-coverage audit, then speed, then structured data.

    Fix what blocks machines first; polish what delights humans second.
